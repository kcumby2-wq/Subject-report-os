# SubjectReport Voice — Writing Skill

## Ecosystem Context

SubjectReport is brand 01 of 3 in the Subject/Ecosystem (Subject·Ecosystem Brand Quick Reference, Vol. 01, 2026, v1.0 — internal, not for external distribution):

| # | Brand | Domain | Accent Color | Brand Line |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 01 | **Subject·Report** | Data · evaluation · placement | Electric blue `#4DB8FF` | "The athlete is not the product. They are the subject." |

SubjectReport never borrows another sister brand's accent color, tone, or "sounds like" line. This file covers SubjectReport (01) only.

## Tone

- **Descriptors:** Educational · analytical · broadcast
- **Sounds like:** the recruiting coordinator who shows up
- **Avoids:** hype, agency-speak, false urgency

An analyst who respects the family's intelligence. Calm, specific, verified. The voice of the one platform in recruiting that is not selling a dream — it is showing the film.

## The Brand Line (use it, live it)

> "The athlete is not the product. They are the subject."

## Patterns That Sound Like SubjectReport

- Leads with the data point: "47 plays graded. Here is what the film actually shows."
- Names the system: Prospect Edge, play-by-play grading, the same platform 350+ coaching staffs use
- Speaks to the parent as the decision-maker, the athlete as the subject of the work
- Frames everything as visibility and truth: "Coaches cannot recruit what they cannot verify."
- Ends with one clear action, never three

## Never

- "Exposure" as a promise — SR sells verified evaluation, not exposure
- Hype words: elite, generational, can't-miss, freak athlete
- Urgency-bro energy, countdown timers, fake scarcity
- Promises about offers, stars, or rankings
- Emoji chains
- Agency-speak or false urgency (ecosystem-wide avoid, doubly true for SR's analytical tone)

## Visual System Reference (for graphics briefs)

**Foundation**
- Deep navy `#051129` (RGB 5 17 41) anchors every surface — never substituted for black or generic dark grays
- Electric blue `#4DB8FF` is SubjectReport's sole accent color; floodlights upper right, frosted glass pill rows

**Typography**
- Display headlines: Barlow Condensed, weight 800, uppercase, -0.005em tracking
- Body + UI: Inter, weight 300/400/500, 15px body, 1.7 leading
- Cormorant Garamond italic is reserved for TOJ Advisory pull-quotes/editorial moments only — never use it for SubjectReport

**Composition — four anchors, always**
1. Top left — Brand
2. Top right — Mark
3. Bottom left — URL (www.SubjectReport.com)
4. Bottom right — CTA

**Special signals (shared ecosystem conventions, used by SubjectReport)**
- **W6 Gold Finale** — Week 6 (education series finale) swaps blue for gold across mark, eyebrow, and headline
- **Tri-color rail** — blue / coral / gold bottom rail signals all three sister brands appear together on one surface; SubjectReport's rail segment is always electric blue
- **Issue/week badge** — numbered framed badge (e.g. "03/06") marks sequenced content; frosted glass, 0.5px accent border

**Other reference points**
- Wordmark renders as **SUBJECT·REPORT** (center dot) in primary use; **SUBJECT/REPORT** appears in the footer — default to the center-dot form unless matching the footer specifically
- Site photography is on-location game/sideline/combine film — verified, unstaged, not stock photography or studio headshots
- Stat chips used as proof points, not decoration: **350+** (coaching staffs on the grading platform), **48h** (film to transcript), **1:1** (placement specialist), **100%** (film-backed grading) — pull from this exact set rather than inventing new stat chips
- The sample transcript document itself is the hero visual whenever a graphic needs to "show the work" — grades + linked clips + plain layout, not a highlight-reel aesthetic

## For Subject Media Cross-Sell Copy

Warmer than standard SR tone. The family just received their transcript. They are emotional and engaged. Lead with the athlete's performance, transition to "now make sure coaches see the full story." Note: Subject Media is a sister brand with its own accent (warm coral `#FF7849`) and tone ("cinematic · fundraising · celebration," sounds like "the team videographer who shows up") — cross-sell copy should warm toward that register without fully adopting it, since the handoff is still being made from SubjectReport.
